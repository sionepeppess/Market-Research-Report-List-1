<p><p>The vehicle tracking market encompasses two primary types: wired and wireless tracking. Wired tracking systems involve the installation of physical cables and devices within the vehicle, providing a reliable and constant power source. In contrast, wireless tracking systems utilize GPS and cellular networks, allowing for easy installation and real-time tracking without the need for extensive wiring. Both types offer unique advantages, with wired systems often seen as more secure, while wireless systems offer flexibility and ease of use for various applications.</p></p>
