<p><p>Modular servers encompass various market types, including Reduced Instruction Set Computing (RISC), System-On-Chip (SoC), and x86 architectures. RISC offers a streamlined instruction set, enhancing efficiency and performance in specific applications. SoC integrates all components onto a single chip, optimizing space and power consumption, ideal for embedded systems. The x86 market, widely used in personal computing and servers, emphasizes compatibility and performance, characterized by complex instruction sets. Each market type caters to distinct use cases, driving innovation in modular server design.</p></p>
