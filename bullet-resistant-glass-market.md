<p><p>The bullet-resistant glass market features several key types: </p><p>1. **Polycarbonate**: A lightweight, shatter-resistant material known for high impact resistance and is often used in security applications. </p><p>2. **Acrylic**: A clear thermoplastic that is lighter and less expensive than glass, offering moderate bullet resistance. </p><p>3. **Glass-Clad Polycarbonate**: Combines layers of glass and polycarbonate for enhanced strength and clarity. </p><p>4. **Poly-Vinyl Butyral (PVB)**: A laminate interlayer used between glass sheets to improve safety and bullet resistance. Each type serves specific security needs in various applications.</p></p>
