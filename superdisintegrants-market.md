<p><p>The superdisintegrants market comprises several types, each enhancing the dissolution of solid dosage forms. Sodium starch glycolate (SSG) is a widely used disintegrant, effective in promoting rapid tablet disintegration. Cross-linked carboxymethyl cellulose (CCS) excels in water absorption, leading to faster breakdown of tablets. Microcrystalline cellulose (MCC) supports the structure while aiding disintegration. Low-substituted hydroxypropyl cellulose (L-HPC) offers improved flow and compressibility for tablet formulations. Other materials include various natural and synthetic polymers that contribute to specific disintegration properties.</p></p>
