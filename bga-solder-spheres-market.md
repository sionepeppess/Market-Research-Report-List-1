<p><p>The BGA solder spheres market primarily comprises two types: lead solder spheres and lead-free solder spheres. Lead solder spheres typically contain a tin-lead alloy and are known for their excellent wetting properties and reliability, though their use is declining due to health and environmental regulations. In contrast, lead-free solder spheres utilize alternative materials, such as tin-copper and tin-silver, to meet safety standards while maintaining high performance in electronic assembly. The shift towards lead-free options is largely driven by regulatory compliance and eco-friendly practices.</p></p>
